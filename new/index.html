About Me

Hi! I'm Catherine, B.S. '14 and M.S. '15 candidate in Computer Science (CS) at Stanford. I'm interested in the intersection between health and technology (software specifically). The US leads the world in health expenditures without necessarily better results. I believe technology can change that, and I intend to use my technical skills to tackle this problem in the future.

I'm pursuing the biocomputation track for my undergraduate CS degree. My coursework differs from other tracks in that it includes courses in the basic sciences and bioinformatics e.g. learning about algorithms in molecular biology, modeling ontologies to make diagnostic decisions, creating systems for data driven medicine, and pursuing team projects in translational bioinformatics.

I'm also working towards a master's degree in CS, dual track in Systems and Artificial Intelligence. I am learning to build robust and efficient large-scale systems, and to gain an understanding in data mining and modeling techniques.

When I'm not working on CS projects or schoolwork, I enjoy playing tennis, watching movies, and exploring new places (I went abroad to Oxford spring 2013, my first time in Europe!). I also love animals--here's a picture of the world's most adorable corgi, Davy (warning: I may be biased).
